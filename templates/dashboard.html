Understanding Brain-Movement Correlation in Fall Prevention
Clinical education · Updated monthly by our medical advisory board
Clinically Validated

The Neuromuscular Loop

The primary motor cortex (M1) generates neural signals that travel down the corticospinal tract to activate skeletal muscles. FallVision measures the temporal coherence between M1 output and actual limb kinematics. When coherence drops — often due to fatigue, medication effects, or early neurodegeneration — limbs begin to deviate from intended trajectories, dramatically increasing fall probability.

Joint Angle Biomechanics

Healthy ambulation requires precise joint angles at each gait cycle phase. Knee extension should reach 170–175° at heel strike; hip flexion should achieve 30–35° at toe-off. Persistent deviations of more than 10° from these normative values correlate with a 3.4× increase in fall incidence (JAMA, 2023). FallVision tracks all four major limb joints simultaneously at 100Hz.

Predictive Risk Modelling

Our composite Fall Risk Score integrates 14 biomechanical variables — including step width variability, double-support time, trunk sway amplitude, and brain-movement coherence — into a single probabilistic score. Validated in a 3,200-patient prospective trial, the model achieves 94.1% sensitivity and 91.3% specificity for fall events occurring within 72 hours (Lancet Digital Health, 2024).
